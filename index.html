Stoic Lessons

A list of lessons from the philosopers Epictetus, Seneca, and Marcus Aurelius

Epictetus

  • Lesson 1

    Our first job is this, to divide and distinguish things into two categories:
    Externals I cannot control, but the choices I make with regard to what I do control. Where will I find good and bad? In me, in my choices.

  • Lesson 2

    It is not events that disturb people, it's thier judgement about things.

  • Lesson 3

    Get rid of self conceit. It is impossible to learn that which one thinks they already know.

  • Lesson 4

    The true man is revealed in difficult times. So when trouble comes, think of yourself as a wrestler paired with a tough young buck. For what purpose? to turn you into olympic-class material.

Seneca

  • Lesson 5

    There are more things likely to frighten us than there are to crush us; we suffer more in imagination than in reality

  • Lesson 6

    Excellence withers without an adversary

  • Lesson 7

    Associate with those who will make a better man of you. Welcome those whom you yourself can improve. The process is mutual; for men learn as they teach.

  • Lesson 8

    This is our big mistake: to think we look forward to death. Most of death is already gone. Whatever time has passed is owned by death.

Marcus Aurelius

The four stoic virtues:
Courage, moderation, justice, wisdom

  • Lesson 9

    At dawn, when you have trouble getting out of bed, tell yourself: "I have to go to work as a human being. What do I have to complain of, if I'm going to do what I was born for; the things I was brought into this world to do. Or is this what I was created for? to huddle under the blankets and stay warm?

  • Lesson 10

    Our actions may be impeded, but there can be no impeding our intentions or our dispositions. Because we can accomodate and adapt. The mind adapts and converts to its own purposes the obstacle to our acting. The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way.

  • Lesson 11

    If, at some point in your life, if you should find anything better than justice, honestly, self-control, and courage, if you find anything better than that, embrace it without reservation. It must be an extraordinary thing indeed.

  • Lesson 12

    No. It's fortunate that this has happened (something perceived as bad) and I've remained unharmed by it-not shattered by the present or frightened of the future. It could have happened to anyone. But not everyone could have remained unharmed by it.

  • Lesson 13

    You could leave life right now. Let that determine what you do and say and think.
